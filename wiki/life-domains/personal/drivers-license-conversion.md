### **Official German Bureaucratic Process for Brazilian License Conversion (Umschreibung)**

#### **Step-by-Step Process at Führerscheinstelle (Driver's License Office)**

**Step 1: Document Preparation**
1. **Brazilian License**: Original + photocopy
2. **Official Translation**: By court-certified translator (öffentlich bestellter Übersetzer)
3. **Eye Test Certificate**: From optometrist (Sehtestbescheinigung)
4. **First Aid Certificate**: 9-hour course (Erste-Hilfe-Kurs)
5. **Biometric Photos**: 35x45mm, recent
6. **Residency Proof**: 
   - German ID card (Personalausweis) or passport
   - Registration certificate (Meldebescheinigung)
   - Permanent residence permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis)
7. **Application Form**: Antrag auf Umschreibung einer ausländischen Fahrerlaubnis

**Step 2: Skill Assessment & Requirement Determination**
1. **Driving School Evaluation**: School assesses your driving skills
2. **Required Training Determination**: Based on:
   - Driving experience (years with Brazilian license)
   - Skill level demonstration
   - Knowledge of German traffic rules
3. **Theory Requirement**: May be reduced or waived if:
   - You pass a knowledge test at driving school
   - Your Brazilian license is recent (within 5 years)
   - You demonstrate good understanding of traffic rules
4. **Practical Training**: Focus on German-specific requirements:
   - Right-before-left rule (Rechts vor Links)
   - Priority roads (Vorfahrt)
   - Environmental zones (Umweltzonen)
   - Speed limits (especially in residential areas)

**Step 3: B197 Automatic-Specific Process**
1. **Automatic-Only Declaration**: You must declare you want B197 restriction
2. **Training Vehicles**: All training and testing in automatic cars
3. **License Code**: B197 will be printed on your German license
4. **Restriction Note**: "78 - nur Fahrzeuge mit Automatikgetriebe" (only automatic vehicles)

**Step 4: Simultaneous A+B Application**
1. **Single Application**: Apply for both classes simultaneously
2. **Combined Documents**: Translation covers both A and B classes
3. **Shared Requirements**: Eye test, first aid course apply to both
4. **Processing Efficiency**: Single bureaucratic process

#### **Official Guidelines for Experience Recognition**
